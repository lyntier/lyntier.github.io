Having my day-to-day structure uprooted in the middle of spring is a little awkward. I have a lot of avenues to explore to help me improve myself. Maybe I'll finally go on that diet, maybe I'll dye my hair a wild color, maybe I'll type daily entries in a blog that I've set up for no reason other than to have a place to type out my thoughts as they bubble up. I used to have a notebook that I treated kind of like a diary, but I hate writing things by hand. I've always grasped pens way too tightly and gotten cramps after a couple minutes of writing. Cursive was never my strong suit in grade school. Why did they even teach us cursive? It's not particularly legible compared to just writing out capital letters. I suppose cursive looks very stylish or whatever, but...

Okay. First things first. The things I always wanted to do, I actually need to take steps to go do them. "I'll regularly exercise" needs to translate to "I _will_ regularly exercise and I _will not_ make excuses to myself to skip out on it". "I'll go on a diet" needs to translate to "I _will_ keep myself to a strict calorie deficit with plenty of fibers and I _will not_ cheat on days that aren't sunday". "I'll play guitar again" needs to translate to "I _will_ make time to practice chords and rhythm every other day and I _will not_ tell myself I'll do it tomorrow instead". You get the point.

Interestingly, I find myself scheduling these things like I would a regular work day. I'm not entirely sure how I feel about that yet. Maybe the planning aspects of my job were never really the problem that I ran into. Doing too much on a single day could prove to be a disastrous point however.

To that end, I suppose a week of mine could be something along the lines of making sure I am actually doing busywork during the nine-to-five (or sometimes shorter, because I appreciate myself and I deserve to take it easy every so often), exercising on monday/wednesday/friday, and playing guitar on tuesday/thursday.

This is of course extremely banal. I'm not factoring in any trips I take, groceries, busywork around the house and any other such appointments I have to go to, but I had to do those things anyway when I was working my nine-to-five. Having the autonomy to organize the times myself feels nicer, and I don't have much of a commute to worry about if I'm working from my own place.

All that's left is figuring out what I'm working on and what kind of story I'm actually trying to tell. Surely that won't be very difficult.
