title: The dream of something better
description: What's next?
date: 2026-04-24
---

Wake up at 6:15AM. Alarm goes off in 15 minutes, there's no way you're going back to sleep. Your sleep was pretty bad too. You sit up, look at the clothes you put on your desk chair the other day. It takes you five- no, ten minutes to stand up. You take a shower, get out the front door. Maybe you get in your car, on your bike, or you're on your way to the bus or train station.

And then, something cracks.

You feel nauseous. Maybe tears start welling up. There's not necessarily any specific thing you could point to that makes you feel this way, but there is _something_ that makes you not want to get in the office today. Having to do the same thing again, having to struggle with the same struggles again. Intern asking you questions that he really should've known about after working here for five months. Support breathing in your neck because your PO decided that they're allowed to sometimes put an emergency story in the sprint (the emergency is, of course, tweaking some text that two users complained about in three months' time).

You feel unwell. Should you call in sick? Should you push through?

Whichever path you choose, maybe you'll feel fine for the next week. Maybe for a month. But then it happens again. You choke up, you're scared. Again you don't have anything in particular that you're concerned about. It's really just... everything. You decide to talk to someone. Maybe your doctor, a family member, a close friend, and they tell you.

"This sounds like you're burning out."

That's not really possible, is it? You're not depressed, you're just a little lazy sometimes. You're a procastinator. You don't _hate_ your work. Your colleagues are tolerable, or at least most of them are.

<hr>

This is what happened to me from August until now. I was constantly teetering on the edge of a burnout and no amount of sick days, time off, or counseling saw any substantial improvement. Every second that I wasn't working or thinking of work, I was focusing on getting better 'so that I could get back to work'. Eventually my boss and I sat down and talked about a settlement agreement. This was a golden opportunity. I saw myself starting for myself without the immediate stress of immediately having to either work for him again, or to find a job again.

Pretty much the exact day after we sat down for a talk, I started working on myself again. If you've been in a weakened mental state before, you know that you start foregoing things. It's refreshing to be thrust into a position where you actually feel like doing the things you haven't been doing. I deep cleaned my entire work area and threw away all the clothes I no longer wore and I felt excited to start exercising again.

Of course this is only initial. I can do a lot of things to improve myself on a physical and mental level, but what's next?

For a very long time I've held the typical dream. I like developing software, I like playing games, _I should like developing games_. I've used several game engines and frameworks in the past for hobby projects, trying to understand stuff. LWJGL as a 17 year old to see how difficult it is to write a Minecraft-style game (note: it is difficult). Unity to see what all the hype is about. Godot because John Riccitiello can't keep his dirty grubby hands out of a model that's actually decent for indie game developers targeting desktops.

I've never really had the kind of revelation or dream of making a specific game that you hear the big indie game developers talk about however. I haven't dreamt of a video game ending that I need to bring into fruition. I'm not Catholic or on the verge of bankruptcy. I _am_ trans but I haven't worked that into any type of storyline about climbing a mountain and facing myself.

I just want to make something enjoyable. What that'll look like is still up in the air. For now this site is a pretty good starting point I think.
